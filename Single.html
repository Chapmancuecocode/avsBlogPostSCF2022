Colorado v St.Louis Game 2

Avs fall 4-1 following a slow start

May 19, 2022 Zach Chapman-Cueco
credit: Getty Images

DENVER-- After a flawless 5 game winning streak. The Avs fall to the St.Louis Blues 4-1 in game 2 of the second round of the Stanley Cup Playoffs. They outscored their opponents 24-11 before succuming to their first defeat. They also lead for 236 minutes during said timeframe, so what was the difference?

Bedsy decided to start the game with the Avs top line against the Blues', a matchup that paid dividends for the Avalanche in game 1. Bit o' foreshadowing, the Blues outshot the Avalanche 14-8 in the first and the 2019 Stanley Cup champions would not step off the gas. At 14:15, of the second, the blues would reap their fruits of labour and convert off a Jordan Kyrou wrist shot taken from above the faceoff dot and beat Kuempy high-glove side to put the Blues ahead 1-0. With 1:28 remaining, Val Choo-Choo-train found himself on a contested break to try and even the game.

"I don't think we played our best game tonight. Obviously we're much better than that"
- Nathan MacKinnon
The big man himself, tried as he might, got a soft fivehole shot away and in the process ran into Jordan Binnington, resulting in a 5-on-3 for St.Louis. They would convert on the opportunity with 33.8 seconds left in the 3rd from a one-timer by Perron to put the Blues up 2-0 after the second.

This was the first time in these Stanley Cup playoffs in 6 games the Avs trailed heading into the 3rd. After a ghost penalty call, and after a deflected pass by Landy, which went right back to him, Gabe the Babe potted the first Avalanche goal in this game on the PP to get the Avs within one with 18:11 left in the 3rd. Erghhmm, 8.5 minutes later Burky and Cale crossed wires, Perron takes the gift, walks in and scores the put-away goal. Rest is history.

The Avs were outshot by 1 (32-31), had the same PP conversion rate, something that did stand out though, blocked shots. Blues had 18 blocked shots this game, which is insane if you think about it. Avs had 5. The Blues are definetly a team that waits to strike, and when they do they strike hard. The avs look to regroup and win game 3 in St.Louis.
